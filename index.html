Why You Should Care

AI in art is not just a technological advancement; it’s a cultural revolution. As AI continues to evolve, it’s reshaping how we think about creativity, originality, and the role of artists in society. This research delves into the implications of AI art, particularly *Edmond de Belamy*, and explores the critical questions it raises about the future of creativity and art markets.

Timeline of AI in Art

  • 1950 - **The Imitation Game**: Alan Turing introduces the Turing Test for machine intelligence.
  • 1973 - **Harold Cohen’s AARON**: One of the first AI programs to create art autonomously.
  • 2014 - **GANs Developed**: Introduction of **Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)** by Ian Goodfellow.
  • 2018 - **Obvious' *Edmond de Belamy* Auction**: AI-generated artwork sold for $432,500 at Christie’s auction house.
